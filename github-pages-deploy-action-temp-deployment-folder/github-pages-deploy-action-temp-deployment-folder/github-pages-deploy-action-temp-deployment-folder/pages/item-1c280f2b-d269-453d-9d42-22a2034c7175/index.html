Area of Specialization

As a Mathematics Education student at Pennsylvania State University, my area of specialization focuses on preparing to teach secondary mathematics (grades 7–12) through equitable, research-based instructional practices. My coursework and field experiences emphasize deep conceptual understanding in algebra, geometry, calculus, and statistics, alongside the pedagogical content knowledge necessary to make these subjects meaningful and accessible to diverse learners.

I am particularly interested in fostering mathematical reasoning, problem-solving, and productive struggle in the classroom. Drawing on current research in mathematics education, I design lessons that promote inquiry, discourse, and real-world connections. I strive to create inclusive learning environments where all students see themselves as capable mathematicians and develop confidence in their quantitative and analytical skills.

Through clinical experiences in local school districts and collaboration with faculty in the College of Education, I continue to refine my instructional strategies, assessment design, and classroom management practices. My goal is to empower students not only to master mathematical content, but to think critically, communicate clearly, and apply mathematics beyond the classroom.
