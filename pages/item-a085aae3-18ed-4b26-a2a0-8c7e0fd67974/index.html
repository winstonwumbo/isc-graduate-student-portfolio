Instructional Practice & Student Engagement

My instructional practice is shaped by extensive experience teaching and supporting students across college mathematics courses and workforce education programs. In these environments, I have worked with learners whose mathematical backgrounds vary widely, requiring flexible and responsive teaching strategies. My goal as an instructor is to create a classroom environment where students are active participants in meaning-making rather than passive recipients of procedures.

Central to my teaching is the use of collaborative learning structures. Drawing on sociocultural learning theory, I design opportunities for students to engage in discussion, explain their reasoning, and build on one another’s ideas. Whether in Supplemental Instruction sessions or formal classroom settings, I use structured group work and guided questioning to support collective problem-solving. These strategies not only improve conceptual understanding but also help students develop confidence in their mathematical voice.

Formative assessment is a key component of my instructional approach. I regularly use low-stakes checks for understanding—such as exit tickets, quick polls, and think-pair-share activities—to gauge student learning and adjust instruction accordingly. This responsiveness allows me to address misconceptions in real time and ensure that lessons remain aligned with student needs.
