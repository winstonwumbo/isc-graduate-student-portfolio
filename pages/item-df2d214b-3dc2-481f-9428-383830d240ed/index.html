Teaching Philosophy

My teaching practice is grounded in a blend of classroom experience, curriculum design, and ongoing scholarly inquiry. As a mathematics instructor within both college courses and workforce development programs, I have taught and supported learners across a wide range of mathematical levels—from developmental arithmetic to calculus. In my roles as a Supplemental Instruction leader, tutor, and curriculum developer, I have designed instructional materials, facilitated collaborative learning environments, and implemented formative assessment strategies that respond in real time to student thinking. These experiences, alongside recognitions such as the Graduate Excellence in Teaching Award, reflect my commitment to both instructional effectiveness and continuous improvement as an educator.

Teaching Demonstrations

Proportional Reasoning in Workforce Contexts

This demonstration is designed for adult learners and focuses on proportional reasoning through applications such as recipe scaling, unit conversions, and material estimation. The lesson begins with intuitive reasoning strategies before introducing formal ratio notation and proportion equations. Students are encouraged to explain their thinking verbally and visually, supporting diverse ways of engaging with the material. The instructional design reflects Universal Design for Learning principles, offering multiple entry points and emphasizing confidence-building alongside skill development.

Function Transformations Using Technology

In this lesson, students explore function transformations using graphing technology to visualize shifts, stretches, and reflections. The demonstration leverages interactive tools to allow students to manipulate parameters and observe real-time changes. Rather than presenting rules upfront, the lesson invites students to notice patterns and develop generalizations. This inquiry-based approach supports deeper conceptual understanding and encourages mathematical curiosity. The lesson also highlights my integration of technology as a tool for exploration rather than passive consumption.
