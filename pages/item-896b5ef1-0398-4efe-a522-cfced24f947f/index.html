My professional expertise spans three interconnected domains: equity-centered mathematics curriculum design, post-secondary and workforce mathematics instruction, and the study of mathematical identity development among non-traditional learners. These areas reflect both the practical knowledge I have built through years of direct instruction and tutoring, and the scholarly grounding I have developed through graduate study at Penn State University.

Curriculum Vitae

Areas of Expertise

  • Equity-Centered Curriculum Design: I design instructional sequences, assessments, and learning materials that center student belonging and access alongside mathematical rigor, with particular focus on developmental and gateway mathematics contexts.
  • Post-Secondary & Workforce Mathematics Instruction: I teach and tutor college mathematics and adult workforce learners across a range of courses from developmental arithmetic through calculus, with expertise in differentiated instruction and formative assessment.
  • Mathematical Identity & Engagement: I investigate how students — particularly adult learners and those in workforce programs — construct or reconstruct their identities as mathematical thinkers, and how instructional environments mediate that process.
  • Pedagogical Content Knowledge: I bring deep command of undergraduate mathematics content (algebra, pre-calculus, statistics, calculus) and the research-informed pedagogical strategies most effective for those domains.
  • Supplemental Instruction & Peer Learning: I design and facilitate peer-assisted learning structures that improve outcomes in high-enrollment gateway courses.
